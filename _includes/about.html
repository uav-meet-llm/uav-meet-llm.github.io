About this workshop

Unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) are now widely deployed in logistics, forestry, and military operations, with multimodal large models driving transformative advances in human-uav collaboration, particularly in visual-language navigation.

This workshop explores cutting-edge integration of UAV systems with large language models (LLMs) through three pivotal innovations: 1) An end-to-end framework converting language commands directly into low-level control signals (velocity, heading), bypassing traditional hierarchical control layers to achieve precise maneuver execution. 2) A cognitive engine enabling multi-level mission breakdown, supporting both single-drone operations and multi-UAV coordination in dynamic scenarios. 3) A digital twin system integrating multimodal data synthesis and physical verification, enabling closed-loop training from language interpretation to flight control. This workshop aims to systematically address technical bottlenecks, chart development roadmaps, and drive paradigm shifts in intelligent UAV systems.

The technical insights and solutions emerging from this workshop are expected to inject new momentum into UAV intelligence, accelerating the evolution of next-generation human-uav collaborative ecosystems.
